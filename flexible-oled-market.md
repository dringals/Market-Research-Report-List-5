<p><ul><li>Crystalline</li><li>Amorphous</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Flexible OLED displays are categorized into two main types based on the structure of the OLED material: Crystalline and Amorphous. Crystalline OLED displays use a structured, ordered arrangement of molecules, which results in higher efficiency and faster response times. On the other hand, Amorphous OLED displays have a random arrangement of molecules, making them more flexible and suitable for curved displays. Both types have their own advantages and applications, with Crystalline OLED being more suitable for high-end devices and Amorphous OLED being more versatile for various form factors.</p></p>
