<p><p>Gas-based extinguishing systems are used to suppress fires by quickly reducing oxygen levels in the area. The main types of gas-based extinguishing systems are clean agent fire suppression systems, CO2 systems, inert gas systems, and others. Clean agent fire suppression systems use chemicals to extinguish fires without leaving residue. CO2 systems flood the area with carbon dioxide to extinguish fires. Inert gas systems use inert gases such as nitrogen and argon to displace oxygen and halt the combustion process. The other category includes hybrid systems and customized solutions.</p></p>
