<p><p>VEGF inhibitor drugs are commonly classified into three types: Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors, Monoclonal Antibodies, and Others. Tyrosine Kinase Inhibitors work by blocking signals that promote the growth of blood vessels, thereby slowing down tumor growth. Monoclonal Antibodies target specific proteins involved in the formation of blood vessels, hindering tumor growth. The "Others" category includes alternative VEGF inhibitor drugs that do not fall into the aforementioned classifications, providing a diverse range of treatment options for various conditions.</p></p>
