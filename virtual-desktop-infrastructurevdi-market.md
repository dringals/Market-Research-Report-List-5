<p><p>Virtual Desktop Infrastructure (VDI) refers to a technology that enables creating virtual desktops on a centralized server, which users can access remotely. The two main market types for VDI are On-Premise VDI and Cloud-Based VDI. On-Premise VDI involves deploying VDI infrastructure within an organization's data center, providing full control and customization. Cloud-Based VDI, on the other hand, allows organizations to access virtual desktops through a cloud service, offering scalability and flexibility without the need for in-house infrastructure maintenance.</p></p>
