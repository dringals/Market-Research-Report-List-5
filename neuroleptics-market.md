<p><p>The neuroleptics market is categorized into three generations based on the development of antipsychotic medications. First-generation neuroleptics, or typical antipsychotics, primarily target dopamine receptors and are effective for positive symptoms of schizophrenia but may cause significant side effects. Second-generation, or atypical antipsychotics, offer broader efficacy for both positive and negative symptoms with a reduced side effect profile. Third-generation antipsychotics focus on modulating dopamine and serotonin systems, aiming for fewer adverse effects and improved patient compliance. Each generation reflects advancements in treatment strategies and patient outcomes.</p></p>
