<p><p>Melt-blown nonwovens are a type of nonwoven fabric that is manufactured using a process called melt-blown technology. This process involves melting a thermoplastic resin and extruding it into thin fibers, which are then stretched and cooled to form a fine web of fibers. Melt-blown nonwovens are known for their high strength, absorbency, and filtration properties, making them suitable for a wide range of applications such as filtration media, medical masks, and wipes.</p><p>The melt-blown nonwovens market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years, with a projected CAGR of 12.7% during the forecast period.
