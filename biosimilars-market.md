<p><p>Biosimilars are classified into two main categories: Recombinant Non-glycosylated Proteins (such as Insulin, rHGH, and Interferon) and Recombinant Glycosylated Proteins. Recombinant Non-glycosylated Proteins are proteins produced by biotechnological processes that lack carbohydrate side chains, whereas Recombinant Glycosylated Proteins are proteins that contain carbohydrate side chains. Both types of biosimilars play a crucial role in the market by offering more affordable treatment options for patients while maintaining similar efficacy and safety profiles as the original biologic drugs.</p></p>
