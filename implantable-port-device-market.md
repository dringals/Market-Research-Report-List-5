<p><p>Implantable port devices are used for long-term access to the bloodstream for administration of medication or fluids. Single lumen implantable ports have one catheter for delivering treatment, while dual lumen implantable ports have two separate catheters for simultaneous administration of medications or fluids. The single lumen ports are typically used for delivering chemotherapy or other medications, while dual lumen ports are commonly used for procedures where blood needs to be drawn or multiple medications need to be administered at the same time.</p></p>
