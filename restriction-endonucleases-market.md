<p><p>Restriction Endonucleases Market can be broadly classified into five types: Type I, Type II, Type III, Type IV, and Others. Type I enzymes recognize a specific DNA sequence but cut randomly away from the recognition site. Type II enzymes are most commonly used in biotechnology due to their ability to recognize specific DNA sequences and cut at precise locations. Type III enzymes require two recognition sites for cleavage while Type IV enzymes target modified DNA. Other types include rare cutting enzymes with unique recognition sequences.</p></p>
