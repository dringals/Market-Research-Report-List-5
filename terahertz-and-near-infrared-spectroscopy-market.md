<p><p>Terahertz and Near-infrared Spectroscopy involves the analysis of materials based on their interaction with near-infrared, mid-infrared, or far-infrared electromagnetic radiation. Near-infrared spectroscopy focuses on wavelengths between 780 to 2500 nm, providing information about chemical composition. Mid-infrared spectroscopy covers wavelengths between 2500 to 25000 nm, giving insights into molecular structure. Far-infrared spectroscopy deals with longer wavelengths beyond 25000 nm, offering details about intermolecular interactions. Each type of spectroscopy has its unique applications and benefits in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, food, and agriculture.</p></p>
